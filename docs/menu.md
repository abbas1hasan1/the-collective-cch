# The Collective — Menu

## Average Ticket
$14–28

## Menu Philosophy
"Every item earned its place on the board. Nothing filler — just fuel, crafted for how CityCentre moves."

## Menu Design Principle
High-protein, high-carb, low-fat fuel for gym-to-office-to-errands workflow. Not indulgence for its own sake — nourishment with purpose. The menu is built around the daily rhythm of CityCentre's active, health-conscious crowd — people flowing between Lifetime Fitness, Nike, Lululemon, Beyond Yoga, pilates studios, pickleball courts, and run clubs. Every item is designed for portability, clean eating, and speed.

---

## 1. Espresso Bar
Lattes, cortados, americanos, single-origin pour-overs, seasonal specials. Foundation of program — every shot pulled with precision.

## 2. Signature Drinks
Matcha lattes, functional beverages, cold brews, specialty iced drinks. Designed to photograph as well as taste.

## 3. Cold Brew Program

The cold brew program is a cornerstone of The Collective's beverage strategy. Cold brew is always on tap — no waiting, no batch delays. The signature lineup includes:

- **Classic Cold Brew** — Smooth, low-acid, always ready
- **Vanilla Cold Brew** — Lightly sweetened with real vanilla
- **Iced Latte** — Espresso-forward, clean finish
- **Caramel Macchiato** — Layered, photogenic, indulgent without the guilt

### Why cold brew matters
Cold brew is the default drink for the active, health-conscious customer. It's low-acid, high-caffeine, and pairs with every lifestyle moment — post-workout, mid-meeting, afternoon pick-up. Having it on tap means zero friction: walk in, grab, go.

### Canned Cold Brew — Portable Branding Play
The same signature cold brews sealed in branded cans for grab-and-go convenience. This is as much a branding play as a product: a matte-finish can designed to look good on a desk, in a gym bag, or on an Instagram story. Every can that leaves the cafe extends the brand's presence into CityCentre sidewalks, offices, and social feeds. The canned line turns the cold brew program into a portable brand ambassador — the same way the matte black cup carrier works for hot drinks.

## 4. Pastries & Bakery
Croissants, danishes, protein muffins, banana oat bars, cookies. Rotating daily from local bakery partners. Fresh every morning with macro-conscious options built into the rotation.

## 5. Collective Cups — Grab & Go Fuel

Curated assortments of fresh fruit, yogurt parfaits, veggie + hummus cups, and cheese + cracker combos. The concept is simple: high-protein, grab-and-go fuel portioned with purpose.

### The "Why" Behind Collective Cups
CityCentre's crowd moves fast — between Lifetime Fitness sessions, office meetings, errands, and pilates classes. They need fuel that fits in one hand and doesn't slow them down. Collective Cups are designed for exactly this rhythm: pick up a cup of fresh fruit or a yogurt parfait on the way out, eat it walking to the car or between sets.

### Portioning Philosophy
Every cup is portioned to be a complete snack — not too much, not too little. The goal is fuel, not a meal. Enough protein and carbs to bridge the gap between workouts and meetings without the heaviness of a full plate.

### Packaging Aesthetic
Clear cups with minimal branding — the food is the visual. Clean labels, earthy tones, consistent with the warm minimalist aesthetic. Designed to look as intentional on a shelf as they taste.

## 6. Breakfast Strategy

Two concepts targeting the post-workout breakfast crowd, both designed for portability and clean eating:

### Toast Builds
Artisan toast builds — breakfast toast, apple toast, berry toast — designed to be clean, portable, and satisfying. High-carb, high-protein starts built for people heading from the gym to the office. The toast program fills the gap between "I need a full breakfast" and "I just want coffee" — it's substantial enough to fuel a morning but light enough to eat on the go.

### Paninis — The Signature Savory
Our signature savory offering — pressed sandwiches with premium ingredients, sealed into a clean, portable shape. No mess, no falling ingredients.

- **Breakfast builds**: Egg white, turkey, avocado — targeting the post-workout crowd heading to the office
- **Lunch builds**: Premium ingredient combos for the midday rush

The panini is the only "made-to-order" savory item on the menu — everything else is grab-and-go. This keeps the line moving while still offering something warm and satisfying. The pressed format is deliberate: it seals everything in, creates a clean portable shape, and eliminates the mess that makes other sandwiches impractical for people on the move.

## 7. Grab & Go Station
The fridge and display case at the front of the store — the "fueling station" concept. Stocked with:
- Overnight oats
- Banana bread
- Power muffins
- Collective Cups (fruit, yogurt, veggie, cheese assortments)
- Bottled cold brew
- Canned cold brew
- Pre-packaged snacks

The grab-and-go station is positioned for maximum speed: walk in, grab, tap, go. No waiting for a barista, no ordering. For the customer who has 90 seconds between parking and their next appointment.

## 8. Coffee Packaging — Walking Billboards

Matte black cup carrier, branded cups — every customer who walks out becomes a walking brand ambassador for CityCentre. The packaging is designed to stand out on every sidewalk, desk, and Instagram story. Minimal, tactile, unmistakable. The cup carrier is the highest-visibility brand asset: it's carried at chest height through CityCentre's promenade, visible to every passerby. This is deliberate — the packaging is as much a marketing investment as it is a functional container.
